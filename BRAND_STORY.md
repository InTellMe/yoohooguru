## Brand Voice Guidelines

### The Sasquatch Tone

Our brand voice should feel like getting advice from a wise, warm, slightly quirky mountain neighbor who happens to be eight feet tall and covered in fur.

**DO:**
- Use warm, conversational language
- Include gentle humor and wordplay
- Reference nature, mountains, and community
- Make technology feel approachable and human
- Acknowledge mistakes with grace and good humor

**DON'T:**
- Be cold, corporate, or distant
- Use aggressive sales language
- Make people feel small or stupid
- Forget the human (or Sasquatch) behind every interaction

### Sample Messages

**Success Messages:**
- "Hot diggity! That worked out great." (Yoohoo)
- "You did it! I knew you had it in you." (Coach)
- "Another connection made, another community strengthened." (Angel)
- "Wonderful! Every step forward matters." (Hero)

**Error Messages:**
- "Well, that didn't go as planned. Let's try a different trail." (Yoohoo)
- "Whoops! Even the best athletes fumble sometimes. Let's reset." (Coach)
- "Something went sideways, but don't worry‚ÄîI'm on it." (Angel)
- "We hit a snag, but no obstacle is too big to overcome together." (Hero)

**Empty States:**
- "Nothing here yet, but that's just room for possibilities!" (Yoohoo)
- "Your roster's empty! Time to build your dream team." (Coach)
- "No listings yet‚Äîbe the first to share your skills!" (Angel)
- "A blank slate means endless opportunity." (Hero)

**404 Page:**
- "Hmm, this trail went cold. Even with our legendary tracking skills, we couldn't find what you're looking for. Let's head back to familiar territory."

## The Secret

The Sasquatch family has kept one secret from the world: **they're not actually hiding anymore.** They've simply discovered that the best way to help humans is to let them think they're helping themselves. The platform does the connecting; the family just makes sure the right people find each other.

As Yoohoo likes to say: *"The best kind of magic is the kind folks don't notice. They just feel lucky."*
